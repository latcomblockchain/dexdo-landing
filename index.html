FAQ

Straight answers.

How is this cheaper than aggregators?

An open order book matches you to the best available ask across sellers, instead of charging a fixed markup on every token. And you get something they don't offer at all — the ability to hedge the price.

Is reselling model access legal?

The buyer-facing marketplace runs on supply whose resale is permitted by provider terms (open models, wholesale, resale-allowed APIs). It's the same category of service as any model router.

What about data and privacy?

DEX.DO inherits Dark Order privacy — shielded prices and order sizes, no front-running — on an anonymous L1 with no account wall. Requests reach the provider directly; the chain only meters usage.

How is it different from other crypto inference markets?

Most are spot-only. DEX.DO adds a secondary market so you can hedge your future cost — plus a private on-chain order book and a rewards program for early participants.

What do I actually pay for?

Access to a model is sold in fixed ticks. You pay per tick as the work streams, so there's nothing to trust up front and your downside is capped.

When does it launch?

The marketplace goes live on testnet first, with test tokens, ahead of mainnet. Join early access to get in with the first cohort.
